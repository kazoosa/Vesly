# Beacon — Impeccable Design Context

## Design Context

### Users

**Primary audience**: Individual retail investors (late-20s to mid-50s) who hold positions across 2–6 brokerages — typical mix is Robinhood for discretionary trades, an employer 401k (Fidelity/Schwab), an IRA at Vanguard or Wealthsimple, and maybe a crypto account at Coinbase. They're technically literate but not developers — they manage their own money seriously but don't have time to reconcile 4 apps and a spreadsheet every week.

**Context of use**:
- **Quick check-in**: Morning coffee, 30 seconds, "how's everything doing?" on phone
- **Deep review**: Weekend on laptop, 15–30 minutes, drill into holdings, rebalance decisions, dividends coming in
- **Tax time**: Once a year, pulling CSV reports for filing
- **Stress moments**: Market down 4%, opens app on phone, wants to see damage + not panic

**Job to be done**: "Show me the real state of my money without me having to do math."

**Tone users expect**: Calm, competent, trustworthy. Same emotional register as a good private banker — not flashy, not condescending, never asking them to "engage" or "crush it." Respect their time and their intelligence.

### Brand Personality

Three words: **grounded · precise · quiet**.

- **Grounded** — Beacon talks about money the way a good CFO does: direct, specific, no hype. Never "unlock your potential," always "here's your unrealized P/L."
- **Precise** — tabular figures, exact dollars, rounded only when it aids readability. Every number is defensible.
- **Quiet** — doesn't shout for attention. The product rewards users who sit with it, not users who scroll fast. Subtle motion, understated colors, confident whitespace.

**Emotional goals**: *confidence* (the numbers are correct), *calm* (nothing is on fire that shouldn't be), *control* (I know exactly where my money is).

### Aesthetic Direction

**Theme**: **Light primary** — serious portfolio tracking happens in daylight, not at 2am. Users check it at breakfast, on a commute, between meetings. Light mode reads as professional and calm, not "tech startup at a hackathon." Dark mode ships as a full peer, not a toggle with different gray blocks — it's for the genuine late-night check-in.

**Reference touchstones**:
- Stripe.com — the measured density, the tabular number treatment, the restraint
- Linear.app — the product's precision, the typographic hierarchy
- Sequoia Capital's quarterly letters — the unshowiness of real money writing about itself
- A classic terminal stock ticker — not retro, but the tabular-figure discipline

**Anti-references**: Mint (dated, cluttered). Robinhood mobile (gamified, emoji-heavy). Most "SaaS dashboard" templates (generic gradient hero + identical card grid + iconed feature row). Any site where the CTA says "let's get started" or "unlock."

**Color**: Warm-tinted neutrals (never pure gray, never pure black). Semantic: emerald for positive P/L, rose for negative, amber for warnings. Accents are rare and earned. No purple-to-blue gradients. No neon.

**Typography**: **Geist** for display & UI (Vercel's geometric sans — it reads as engineered without being cold), **JetBrains Mono** for every number, every ticker, every money value. The mono treatment for numbers is the signature detail; tabular figures align across rows so columns of dollars read cleanly.

### Design Principles

1. **Numbers are the brand.** Typography for money is more important than any logo. Tabular figures everywhere, no exceptions.

2. **Whitespace is not wasted space.** Density earns its place through hierarchy. Where we could add a decoration, we add padding instead.

3. **Motion should inform, not perform.** State transitions (enter/exit), feedback (tap, hover), and hierarchy reveals — those are welcome. Decorative bounces, parallax, scroll-triggered pageants — never. Respect `prefers-reduced-motion` always.

4. **Consistency over cleverness.** Same thing called the same thing everywhere. Same spacing units everywhere. Same button styles everywhere. Clever one-off UI in one place is noise; restraint is the product.

5. **Respect the money.** No dark patterns. No "upgrade to see your data" locks. Users can export their full portfolio and delete their account in one click. The product's trustworthiness comes from never making them question it.

### Accessibility

- **WCAG 2.1 AA** across the board.
- All text ≥ 4.5:1 against its background. Muted text ≥ 3:1.
- Every interactive element has a visible `:focus-visible` state — never remove the outline without replacement.
- Touch targets ≥ 44×44px on mobile.
- Color is never the only signal (P/L deltas pair ± signs with color).
- `prefers-reduced-motion` honored in all transitions.

### Device Reach

Launch: **responsive web** — mobile (360px+) through desktop (1920px+). PWA-capable so iPhone/Android users can "Add to Home Screen" and get app-like behavior. No native apps day one — but every layout decision respects the possibility of a future React Native port, so sidebar → bottom tab, card grid → stacked rows, hover states paired with tap equivalents.

### Ship Bar

**Flagship, not MVP.** Beacon is a paid product competing against Snowball ($8) and Monarch ($15). It cannot feel like a side project. Every screen is held to the same bar: one inconsistency or rough edge undermines every other screen.
